The 'art' part of the art page

I draw pretty much entirely with traditional tools. I start with a hard H2 pencil, then usually do a degree of detailing with a mechanical HB pencil, then more added detailing and shading with a B2 or B3 pencil. For paper I use whichever art sketchbook of sufficiently heavy and thick drawing paper I have last come across. The pencil process can take anywhere between half an hour and six hours, depending on the level of finishing.

If I intend to colour a picture, I start by meticulously inking all of the lines, then erasing all the pencil once I am done, then continuing inking even further. I tend to add a lot of details at this part. After inks it's time to bring out the colour pencils and promarkers.

"Where do you get your ideas?", one might ask... and have asked, plenty of times. To which I can only really say 'everything around me', everything from what I read and write and see and watch and observe. There is never a single source for where an idea comes from, it's almost always a combination of several things in my mind.

My preferences are very firmly in the furry and feminine and curvy and pointlessly detailed, pretty traditional furry art in a lot of ways. I draw what I like, heh.
